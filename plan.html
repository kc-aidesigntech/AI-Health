Our Implementation Plan

A phased approach to building a sustainable healthcare solution for Anderson Island.

COMPLETED

Phase 1: Assess Community Needs

The first step was to listen to the community. We conducted a comprehensive needs assessment and hosted forums to gain support and gather responses. This data is the foundation of our entire plan.

IN PROCESS (0-6 Months)

Phase 2: Foundation & Immediate Access

We are currently working on the foundational elements required to launch a clinic. Key activities include:

  • Continuing 'Share and Care' monthly blood pressure/check-in events.
  • Working with Pierce County to secure land and zoning approval.
  • Pursuing partnerships with regional healthcare providers.
  • Coordinating with EMTs for volunteer wellness visits.
FUTURE (6-18 Months)

Phase 3: Mobile & Rotating Care Model

This is the cornerstone of our vision. We will launch a capital campaign to fundraise $350,000 to acquire and equip a mobile medical clinic. This mobile hub will be parked at a central location and will:

  • Offer full-spectrum primary care 3 days a week to start.
  • Provide walk-in services and mental health support.
  • Deliver services like chronic disease management, immunizations, and basic lab work.
  • Host visiting specialty providers (e.g., dentist, cardiologist).
  • Hire permanent staff, including a primary care provider, nurse, and clinic coordinator.
LONG-TERM (18-36 Months)

Phase 4: Expand Partnerships

To ensure long-term sustainability and comprehensive care, we will expand our partnerships, particularly with local EMS. The goal is to create an Integrated Mobile Health Model, similar to Mason County's, where a PA/ARNP can join EMS calls to reduce unnecessary emergency calls and hospital readmissions.
