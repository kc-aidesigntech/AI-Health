Our Plans

Our roadmap translates community survey results into tangible milestones—moving from data, to zoning, to a fully equipped mobile clinic serving every household.

From insight to action

What residents told us

  • 84% rely on primary care off-island today—so we prioritize on-island appointments and telehealth support.
  • 72% feel the ferry is a barrier—which is why we are investing in mobile services and visiting specialists.
  • 81% would use walk-in care immediately, underscoring the need for rapid deployment of urgent support.

Implementation timeline

Completed

Phase 1 · Assess Community Needs

Completed surveys, listening sessions, and forums established the baseline for services and funding strategies.

In Progress · 0-6 months

Phase 2 · Foundation & Immediate Access

  • Continue "Share and Care" wellness drop-ins and home visits.
  • Finalize land use and zoning alignment with Pierce County.
  • Secure regional provider MOUs and volunteer coverage.
Next · 6-18 months

Phase 3 · Mobile & Rotating Care Model

Launch a capital campaign to fundraise $350,000 for a mobile clinic equipped for primary care, diagnostics, and telehealth.

  • Staff the unit with a primary care provider, nurse, and clinic coordinator.
  • Offer walk-in hours three days per week with lab, pharmacy, and mental health access.
  • Host rotating specialists to match top survey requests.
Long-Term · 18-36 months

Phase 4 · Integrated Partnerships

Develop an Integrated Mobile Health model with EMS partners so advanced practice clinicians can respond alongside medics, reducing unnecessary emergency transports.
