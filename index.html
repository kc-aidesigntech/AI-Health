Addressing a Critical Healthcare Gap

Anderson Island is a close-knit community of approximately 1,500 year-round residents, accessible only by ferry. Currently, all basic healthcare services—even simple lab tests or minor urgent care—require a time-consuming, expensive, and sometimes unreliable ferry trip to the mainland.

Our Vision: A Mobile Clinic for the Island

Building on strong community feedback, Anderson Island Health (AIH) is moving quickly to explore a mobile healthcare unit as the next step in making this vision a reality. This clinic will function as a vital healthcare hub, offering primary care, walk-in services, lab work, and more.

Our immediate goal is to fundraise $350,000 to acquire, convert, and equip a mobile medical clinic vehicle, making healthcare directly accessible to our community.
