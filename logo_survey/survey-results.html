Design Guardrails

Lean into

  • Island silhouettes or interconnected forms that symbolize belonging and shared care.
  • Calming gradients that transition from turquoise to soft coral, mirroring local sunrise light.
  • Clean, humanist typography that can stretch from signage to digital touchpoints.

Avoid

  • Traditional medical crosses that feel generic rather than community-built.
  • Illustrations of ferry boats or literal transport imagery.
  • Highly specific flora or fauna that leave some neighbors out of the story.

Strategic Conclusions

The logo should embody a neighbor-first health experience—calm, collaborative, and unmistakably Anderson Island.

  • Lead with connection. Design around shared humanity—interlocking shapes or layered gradients that feel like people coming together.
  • Balance calm with momentum. Pair gentle blues and teals with a spark of coral to suggest proactive care without sacrificing serenity.
  • Showcase island warmth. Use rounded letterforms, approachable spacing, and subtle landscape cues to emphasize local pride.
  • Create room for storytelling. Ensure the logo system scales into sub-marks and campaign graphics that highlight programs, volunteers, and donors.
