Implementation Timeline
- Eyebrow: Implementation timeline
- Phase 1 — Completed — Assess Community Needs — Completed surveys, listening sessions, and forums established the baseline for services and funding strategies.
- Phase 2 — In Progress · 0-6 months — Foundation & Immediate Access
  - Continue “Share and Care” wellness drop-ins and home visits.
  - Finalize land use and zoning alignment with Pierce County.
  - Secure regional provider MOUs and volunteer coverage.
- Phase 3 — Next · 6-18 months — Mobile & Rotating Care Model
  - Launch a capital campaign to fundraise $350,000 for a mobile clinic equipped for primary care, diagnostics, and telehealth.
  - Staff the unit with a primary care provider, nurse, and clinic coordinator.
  - Offer walk-in hours three days per week with lab, pharmacy, and mental health access.
  - Host rotating specialists to match top survey requests.
- Phase 4 — Long-Term · 18-36 months — Integrated Partnerships
  - Develop an Integrated Mobile Health model with EMS partners so advanced practice clinicians can respond alongside medics, reducing unnecessary emergency transports.

  - Future Fixed Site — Zoning updates and long-term capital planning create room for a permanent clinic as demand grows.

Title Page
- Title: Anderson Island Health Logo Survey
- Lede: Neighbors shared how the refreshed identity should feel—grounded in care, connection, and the calm of island life.

Community Pulse Section
- Heading: Community Pulse
- Intro: Residents emphasized a welcoming, community-forward story that celebrates shared care and trust on Anderson Island.
- Card 1 — Care & Compassion — Care, compassion, and comfort were the most repeated words, signaling that the logo must instantly feel supportive. Badges: Care; Compassion; Comfort.
- Card 2 — Community Connection — Neighbors described the brand as an emblem of belonging—reflecting an island network that looks after one another. Badges: Community; Warmth; Advocacy.
- Card 3 — Trust & Reliability — Trust and reliability were identified as the primary message, reinforcing the need for confident, timeless typography and mark-making. Badges: Trust; Reliability; Service.

Visual Priorities Section
- Heading: Visual Priorities
- Intro: Participants outlined the ingredients that should appear in the final logo and how the new system should balance modern energy with enduring credibility.
- Subheading: Elements that matter most
  - Sense of community & connection — 94%
  - Calm, serene feeling — 89%
  - Natural landscape cues — 86%
  - Movement or journey motifs — 61%
  - Historical references — 38%
- Footnote: Percent of respondents rating each element as “essential” or “very important.”
- Subheading: Style direction & palette
  - Statement: Respondents prefer a friendly, timeless look accented by luminous island hues and welcoming typography.
  - Badges: Friendly & inviting; Comforting; Approachable.
  - Palette Descriptions
    - Ocean Blues — Signal trust, professionalism, and the surrounding waters that define island life.
    - Evergreen Teal — Represents natural wellness, volunteer spirit, and year-round vitality.
    - Sunrise Coral — Introduces warmth and optimism, echoing the rosy highlight seen in the selected mark.
    - Peach Light — Softens gradients and adds approachability when paired with the cooler tones.

Design Guardrails Section
- Heading: Design Guardrails
- Column 1 — Lean into
  - Island silhouettes or interconnected forms that symbolize belonging and shared care.
  - Calming gradients that transition from turquoise to soft coral, mirroring local sunrise light.
  - Clean, humanist typography that can stretch from signage to digital touchpoints.
- Column 2 — Avoid
  - Traditional medical crosses that feel generic rather than community-built.
  - Illustrations of ferry boats or literal transport imagery.
  - Highly specific flora or fauna that leave some neighbors out of the story.

Strategic Conclusions
- Heading: Strategic Conclusions
- Paragraph: The logo should embody a neighbor-first health experience—calm, collaborative, and unmistakably Anderson Island.
- Bullet Points
  - Lead with connection. Design around shared humanity—interlocking shapes or layered gradients that feel like people coming together.
  - Balance calm with momentum. Pair gentle blues and teals with a spark of coral to suggest proactive care without sacrificing serenity.
  - Showcase island warmth. Use rounded letterforms, approachable spacing, and subtle landscape cues to emphasize local pride.
  - Create room for storytelling. Ensure the logo system scales into sub-marks and campaign graphics that highlight programs, volunteers, and donors.
